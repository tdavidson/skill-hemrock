# Sanity checks — Unit Economics Tool


## Validate CAC definition

Confirm what is included in the CAC numerator: paid spend only, paid + organic, paid + sales + marketing salaries, or fully-loaded? Different definitions produce CAC numbers that can differ by 2–3x. State the definition explicitly before quoting LTV/CAC.

## Validate payback uses contribution margin not revenue

Confirm that the CAC payback period is calculated on contribution margin, not on revenue. Revenue payback is meaningless for businesses with significant COGS or variable costs. If the model is using revenue, the payback number is overstated.

## Validate LTV horizon is stated

Confirm the LTV calculation explicitly states (1) the time horizon (e.g., 24-month, lifetime), (2) the retention assumption used, and (3) the gross margin applied. An LTV without these three is not a meaningful number.

## Validate LTV/CAC benchmark in context

Before applying a benchmark like 'LTV/CAC > 3 is healthy', confirm what stage and business type the benchmark assumes. A 3x ratio is healthy for a venture-backed company with 12-month payback, but the bar should be higher for high-margin SaaS or lower for capital-intensive businesses. State the context.
